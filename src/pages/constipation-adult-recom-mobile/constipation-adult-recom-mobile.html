Constipation can be painful, let's see what to use over-the-counter.

Over the counter medications or measures that can help with constipation are listed below.

Go through them. You will then answer a few questions to see which will work best for your situation.

If still not satisfied, there is a comparison table at the end of the questions to further tailor the recommendation to your needs. 



Stool softeners click to expand contents

This allows more water and fat to get into the stool making it softer and easier to move along.

They are stimulant free and generally regarded as gentle.

But expect action in about 1 to 3 days.

Docusate is a good example.

 

Bulk-forming fibers click to expand contents

These cause the stool to retain more water, bulk it up and form something like a gel making it easier to move along.

They are gentle and as such can be used daily to keep things moving.

Some of these fibers are sources of soluble and insoluble fibers. While the insoluble fibers help make things move in the intestine, the soluble fibers have been shown to reduce cholesterol and ultimately heart disease.

In 1998, the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) authorized the use of a health claim that ...diets low in saturated fat and cholesterol that include 7 grams of soluble fiber per day from psyllium may reduce the risk of heart disease by lowering cholesterol... in the labeling of foods and dietary supplements containing psyllium husk.     

If you have a history of constipation, you should include this in your daily routine.       

Examples include:  Psyllium, cellulose, methylcellulose, carboxymethylcellulose sodium, karaya, malt soup extract, polycarbophil, and wheat bran.

 

 

 

Stimulants click to expand contents

These stimulates or make the intestine more active thereby causing a bowel movement.

They act on the walls and nerves of the intestine, altering the electrolytes and water movement.

They are the most powerful among laxatives, but also the most dangerous.

For instance, prolonged use can damage the ability of the folds in the colon to move stool along on its own because you've over stimulated it.

Then you will need to take another stimulant laxative to move things along and before you know it, you will end up with chronic constipation that is dependent on you having to take these laxatives before you can go. In other words, you will become dependent on them.

So use these on a short term basis. Never more than a week.

Examples are senna extracts, castor oil, bisacodyl, cascara, aloe vera, buckthorn. 

 

Lubricants click to expand contents

The idea is to make the stool slippery so it can pass out easily.

A good example is mineral oil. 

Being an oil, mineral oil, also add more water to the stool making it softer, because it prevents the colon from absorbing water.

Only problem is that it will also prevent the absorption of fat-soluble vitamins and some minerals by dissolving them in the oil and passing them out.

 

Saline laxatives click to expand contents

These draws water into the intestine and retain it to make stool softer and more comfortable to pass.

You should take this with a full glass of water not only to make it more effective, but  also prevent side effects like dehydration or electrolyte imbalance.

Examples include magnesium citrate, magnesium hydroxide or milk of magnesia, magnesium sulfate and sodium phosphate.

Stay away from oral sodium phosphates. These when taken at high doses  in some individuals carry a risk of kidney injury. For this reason Fleet phospho-soda  (Oral form. Not enema)  was recalled by the manufacturer.

 

Hyper-osmotic agents click to expand contents

These cause the intestine to hold more water within thereby soften the stool for easy passage.

Their goal is to hydrate the stool, soften it and make it easier to pass.

Examples include glycerin suppository, polyethylene glycol (PEG), sorbitol, lactulose.

Something to prevent?

 

Preventive measures click to collapse contents


1. Include enough fiber in the diet

2. Drink fluids more often

3. Exercise often

4. Avoid sitting in one place for too long. If on a long journey, find time to stretch and walk around.
