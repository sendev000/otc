What could be the cause?
Dry mouth is unknown for some people, but to you it is killing you.

Food is difficult to swallow, seem dry, not as tasteful as you would expect because of a dry tongue.

And since saliva is in short supply, some enzymes that aid in digestion or even saliva that help to prevent tooth decay will be lacking.

Have you been diagnosed with dry mouth (xerostomia)?

Or this is your first time feeling your mouth being dry?

First time Diagnosed

 

Alright,
let's be sure you are looking at a dry mouth.

Lack of saliva which moistens the mouth and help with digestion will make your mouth feel dry.

 

Does it feel like your saliva is thick and stringy?

 

Yes?
Dryness of the mouth can make it feel this way.

 

Does the tongue feel dry to you?

 

Yes?
This is a symptom of a dry mouth. The tongue feels dry.

 

Does food feel dry to you?

 

Yes?
With a dry mouth food will feel dry. This is a symptom.

 

Are the corners of the mouth splitting?

 

They are?
With dry mouth the corners of the mouth start splitting.

Dry mouth symptoms include:

  • Thick and Stringy saliva
  • Tongue feeling dry
  • Food feeling dry
  • Corners of mouth splitting

 

Are the lips cracking?

 

Yes?
Absence of saliva to moisten the lips will make them crack.

Symptoms include:

  • Thick and Stringy saliva
  • Tongue feeling dry
  • Food feeling dry
  • Corners of mouth splitting
  • Cracking lips

 

Are you losing taste sensations?

 

Yes?
This is a symptom of a dry mouth.

Symptoms include:

  • Thick and Stringy saliva
  • Tongue feeling dry
  • Food feeling dry
  • Corners of mouth splitting
  • Cracking lips
  • Loss of taste sensations

 

Are you having difficulty speaking?

 

Yes?
With a dry mouth, you will have difficulty speaking.

 

Do you think you have dry mouth because...

...food is hard to swallow?

 

 

Are you experiencing more tooth decaying?

 

Yes?
Tooth decay usually accompany dryness of the mouth.

If you are also experiencing other symptoms described above, discuss this with your dentist or doctor.

 

Do you frequently experience soreness of the throat?

 

You do?
Saliva not reaching the throat to moisturize it will make it sore.

Are you currently taking prescription medications?

Most prescription medications can cause dry mouth.

 

Yes?
Call the pharmacy and see if it has dry mouth as a side effect and then discuss this with your doctor.

Do not stop taking the medication without your doctor's knowledge.
