Let's see how to take care of this insect bite.

Know that we are talking about insect bites and stings that caused a mild reaction confined to the area of the bite.

If you are having a severe reaction to the insect bite, seek emergency room visit.

Since most insect bites cause itching and swelling, we will take a look at what is available over the counter to take care of this.

We will also figure out what to do in case there are other symptoms like pain or blisters.

With this in mind, use the search field below to search for an insect and see what is available over the counter to take care of the bites.

Self-care recommendations for bedbugs bites are discussed below. But you must have pest control get rid of these bedbugs to be successful.

Anti-Itch click to expand contents

Diphenhydramine

You will take the tablets to help with itching if the affected area is pretty large. If not a large area, the cream, roll-on or spray will do fine.

Calamine Lotion    ​

This is good as an anti-itch, antiseptic to prevent infection and astringent to dry up the affected spots.

Hydrocortisone 1%    

This is a steroidal cream that helps relieve itching and inflammation. Use this if the affected area is not large and dab it on the spots rather than rubbing all over the skin. You want to minimize the amount that the skin will absorb.

 

Anti-inflammation click to expand contents

Hydrocortisone 1%    

This will help both with the inflammation and itching of the affected area. Do not apply to a very large continuous  area. If the bedbugs bite area is large, you have to seek urgent care.

NSAIDs

Ibuprofen or naproxen will help if the bites are painful or very inflamed. 

Witch Hazel

Consider dabbing witch hazel on the affected areas if it is oozing. Probably use this before applying hydrocortisone cream or lotion.

 

Worth Doing click to expand contents

1. Must get pest control company to get rid of the bedbugs. Do not try to do this yourself. You won't be successful. DIY does not work here.

2. Vacuum mattress and box-spring crevices

3. Place beddings and clothes in the dryer for 20 minutes on medium to high heat.

4. Cover mattress and box springs with a mattress cover that is impermeable.

Remedies available over-the-counter for bee stings are discussed below.

Remove the Stinger click to expand contents

This is the first step you have to take. It does not matter how you remove it, just do it. The earlier the better. A delay of a few seconds means more venom being injected.

 

Pain Relief click to expand contents

Cold Compress

Do this once the stinger has been removed. This will help reduce the inflammation or swelling. Keep applying ice until the pain has subsided. While doing this, have someone go to the pharmacy to get you benzocaine.

Benzocaine, Lidocaine

These are local anesthetics that will numb the area providing pain and itch relief. Look for Lanacaine, Solarcaine sprays. Or go to the pharmacy counter and ask for lidocaine gel/cream. Any percentage is adequate, but the higher the better. LMX is a good brand. If these are not available, apply Orajel or Anbesol used for a toothache if you have any at home.

 

Inflammation click to expand contents

Hydrocortisone 1%

This should help with the swelling, redness and itch. Use this after using ice to reduce the pain.

NSAIDs

Take aspirin to help with the inflammation if you can, especially to reduce the redness. Prefer aspirin over ibuprofen or naproxen but either should be fine.

 

Anti-Itch click to expand contents

Diphenhydramine

Take the tablets to help with the itch or prevent excessive itching. You will also take this to prevent any allergic reaction to the bee sting.

You may combine this hydrocortisone 1% cream or lotion for  inflammation that can cause redness, swelling and itching. 

You must not scratch the area.

Remedies for chigger bites available over-the-counter are discussed below.

For Itching click to expand contents

1. Cold / Ice

Apply this to the affected area to start with.

2. Hydrocortisone 1%

Will help with the itching and inflammation

3. Capsaicin cream

4. Heat the bite with a hair dryer    

5. Add 2 tablespoonful salt in a jar of Vicks Vapor rub.

Over-the-counter recommendations for mosquito bites are discussed below.

Anti-Itch click to expand contents

Hydrocortisone 1%

Apply this for both itching and to get rid of the swelling

Antihistamine

Take the tablets if the bites are all over the body. Diphenhydramine is a good example. The creams, sprays or lotions are better used if the bites just happened. These can prevent further release of histamine that causes the itching.

Calamine Lotion

This is useful to stop the itching and being an astringent, can reduce swelling.

Combination

Hydrocortisone 1% + Diphenhydramine (tablets or cream)

 

For inflammation click to expand contents

Cold Compress / Ice    ​

Use this as soon as possible to control any swelling.

Hydrocortisone 1%

Apply this to prevent or reduce swelling and redness of the affected area.
