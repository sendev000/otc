First, we will start by eliminating serious causes of leg or foot pain that will require urgent or emergency care.

 

Do you have foot pain that became severe within seconds or minutes?

Particularly if you have a history of heart problems.

 

Seek emergency room or call your doctor.

Here is why:

  • You might be looking at something serious like an embolism of one of the arteries that supply blood to the foot.
  • Especially so if your foot feels cool.

 

Is your toe, foot or ankle warm to touch, red and painful?

Or very tender?
(covering it with blanket hurts.)

 

Seek urgent care or call your doctor.

Here is why:

  • May be looking at an acute attack of gout.
  • The pain starts suddenly, often during the night and is often described as throbbing, crushing, or excruciating.

 

Does the foot feel numb, burning , tingling?

Like a deep pain in there?

 

Seek urgent care or call the doctor.

Here is why:

  • You may be looking at peripheral neuropathy involving the legs.
  • Must see the doctor if you are diabetic.

 

Are the feet and toes swollen, feeling numb?

Painful at the ankle and heel?

 

Call your doctor.

Here is why:

  • May be looking at hypothyroidism.

 

When you take off your shoes, do you feel a throbbing heel pain?

With heel puffy and warm?

 

See a doctor.

Here is why:

  • Possibly calcaneal bursitis.
