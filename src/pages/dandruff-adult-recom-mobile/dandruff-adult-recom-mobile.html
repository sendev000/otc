Let's see what to use over-the-counter for dandruff.

With dandruff, the goal is to control it. You may have to try different things before you find what is working for you.

You will also need patience and stay with a medication to be able to keep it under control.
For instance, medications like salicylic acid act by causing the outer layer of the skin on the scalp to loosen and shed.

When this happens, the dandruff will obviously get worse for the first 1 or 2 weeks before it gets better.

If you are not patient, you will get discouraged and abandon this therapy. 

Alright, let's start by taking a look at how the medications available over-the-counter for dandruff works.

This will give you enough information to take control of things.  



Antifungal agents click to expand contents

These serve to remove or reduce yeast or the fungus (Malassezia furfur) on the scalp that can cause dandruff.

Examples include ketoconazole, pyrithione zinc, selenium sulfide shampoos.

 

Antibacterial click to expand contents

They reduce bacterial count on the scalp that can hasten the death of skin cells of the scalp.

Example include Zinc pyrithione (ZPT).

 

Keratolytic agents click to expand contents

Causes the outer layer of the skin to soften, loosen and shed thereby reducing skin flakes and scales.

Examples include salicylic acid, sulfur.

 

keratoplastics click to expand contents

Slows down the over production of skin cells on the scalp.

Slows the death and flaking off of skin cells on the scalp. 

Examples include coal tar, salicylic acid, sulfur.

Preventive measures click to collapse contents


Use a gentle shampoo daily to prevent oiliness and flaky build up.

Use an antifungal shampoo like ketoconazole or selenium sulfide once or twice a week to control the fungal count on the scalp

Managing a mild case. click to expand contents

Start with:

Salicylic acid + sulfur or coal tar.

You need to soften the outer layer of the skin, loosen it and make it shed. 

This should reduce skin flakes and scales.

Then:    

Use zinc pyrithione for control. This should keep the bacteria count on the scalp in check. Bacteria can hasten the death of skin cells of the scalp.

Or if not too bad, use emollients like lanolin to keep the scalp hydrated.

 

 

Managing a moderate case. click to expand contents

Start with:

Salicylic acid, sulfur or coal tar to soften,

 loosen the outer layer of the skin on the scalp. You need to reduce skin flakes and scales.

Then:

Once you are confident the outer skin layer is cleared, use an antifungal agent like selenium sulfide or ketoconazole to keep the fungi population in check. 2 weeks of use should take care of this.

You can then maintain hydration with an emollient like lanolin.

 

 

Approach to severe cases. click to expand contents

Start by

Taking out the top dead cells layer of the scalp.

This will allow other medications to penetrate the skin and act.

To do this, you will use salicylic acid, sulfur or coal tar for about 2 weeks.

Know that it is going to appear worse because these agents are scalp scrubbers.

After 2 weeks

Switch to ketoconazole, selenium sulfide or  zinc pyrithione containing medications to kill or reduce the fungi or yeast population on the scalp for another 2 weeks.

Then

Use emollients like lanolin to help keep the scalp hydrated.

 

 

Oily, dry, blonde or colored? click to expand contents

Oily?

Prefer salicylic acid and or coal tar for taking out the flakes and scales.

Dry?

If the scalp is dry, stay away from these two. They will make things worse.

Blonde, chemical or color treated?

Avoid selenium sulfide. Go with ketoconazole or zinc pyrithione instead.

 

 

See A Doctor. click to expand contents

You will have to see a doctor.

Looking at serious inflammation that will require the use of topical steroids which are not available over-the-counter.  

May also be looking at possible scalp infection that will require a good evaluation by the physician.

 

 

Worth doing click to expand contents

1. Keep in mind that dandruff is a chronic condition. It cannot be cured. Your focus should be on control.

Once under control, focus on maintenance to prevent it from getting worse.
